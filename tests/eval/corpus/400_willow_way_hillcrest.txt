Engineering Report - 400 Willow Way, Hillcrest, FL 30004

Property Overview
The subject property is a single-family concrete block residence with a low-slope roof over the rear lanai and a shingled main roof. The inspection addressed damage reported after the windstorm.

Roof and Envelope Observations
Wind damage was observed to the ridge and field shingles on the eastern slope. A storm-created opening was confirmed where shingles and underlayment were displaced, permitting water entry.

Interior Observations
The interior of the residence sustained water intrusion from the openings in the roof caused by the storm. Floor tiles in the family room were not adhering properly, and cracking was present in both the ceiling and the walls of the adjoining hallway.

Moisture readings taken at the affected drywall were elevated relative to unaffected baseline areas, confirming recent water intrusion rather than historical staining.

Conclusion
Based on the field observations and moisture data, the interior damage is the result of water that entered through storm-created openings in the roof. The damage pattern is consistent with a discrete weather event.
