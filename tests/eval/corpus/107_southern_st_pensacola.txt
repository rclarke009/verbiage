Engineering Report - 107 Southern Street, Pensacola, FL 32503

Property Overview
The subject property is a single-story masonry residence with a hip roof covered by asphalt three-tab shingles. The inspection was performed to assess reported damage following the named windstorm event.

Roof Observations
High-velocity winds removed and tore shingles along the south-facing slope, creating a storm-created opening in the roof covering. The exposed underlayment allowed wind-driven rain to enter the attic and travel into the living space below.

Interior Observations
In the primary bedroom, a crack was observed between the crown molding and the wall. This cracking is consistent with water intrusion that entered through the storm-created opening in the roof. Moisture staining was present on the adjacent ceiling.

In the hall bathroom, cracking was observed at the joint between the window frame and the wall. The pattern of staining indicates water that entered through the storm-created opening migrated down the wall cavity.

Conclusion
It is our professional opinion that the interior water damage at this residence is the direct result of wind-driven rain entering through a storm-created opening in the roof. The damage is consistent with a single windstorm event and is not the result of long-term wear or deferred maintenance.
